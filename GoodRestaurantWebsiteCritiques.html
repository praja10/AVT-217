Good Restaurant Website Critiques

Mellow Mushroom

This is a good website that follows the grid rule and makes it easy to see their large menu. Their organization for each menu category is visually appealing and easy to scroll through. I would recommend pictures of each food item but since their menu is so large, it makes sense not to include it since it could be overwhelming. Their large order online button at the bottom right increases easy navigation as well.

Quickfire Hibachi

Quickfire uses a simple but effective scheme for their website. They stick to their color of red and white that emphasizes the colors of the food they have. Their menu is designed to look like a paper menu, making it easy to navigate through. Contact info, franchise info and loyalty programs are also included in clear and concise ways for maximum accessibility.

Cava

Another website that effectively uses the grid rule. Their menu is simple and clean and each menu item is perfectly separated with hero images depicting each genre. The dark color scheme makes their bright, colorful food pop even more.

Crumbl Cookies

Crumbl’s black and white theme is very cohesive and clean. It makes all the images of their cookies stand out. A feature that I liked is that they used hero videos along with pictures to make their website a more interactive experience.

Château de Chantilly

This website is unique in that the main page is a hero video that takes up the whole screen. However, once you click on the Order Online option, you can see their menu. Using the grid theme and having a small picture next to every menu item makes it very visually appealing while also providing a strong user experience. I often wish I could see what food looks like on the menu and this will certainly help people in making their decisions.
